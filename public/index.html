What's this all about?

The Eurovision Song Contest (ESC or just Eurovision) is a yearly, international song competition between members of the European Broadcasting Union. Each broadcaster internally chooses both a song and a performer to send to Eurovision, typically by a professional jury or a televised public vote (or both). More from eurovision.tv

Since voting in the Contest is only available to viewers in participating member countries, I've built this little application to allow fans in non-EBU nations to be able to hold their own, unofficial vote.

Its all for fun, folk! Numbers in this app are in no way connected to, nor will they reflect officially cast votes.

More about Eurovision \/

  • The first Eurovision Song Contest was held in 1956 between 7 nations.
  • The Contest is (typically) hosted/broadcast/produced by the broadcaster from previous year's winner's country. The 2019 contest is in Tel Aviv and produced by KAN as Israel won in 2018.
  • In 2019, participants from 42 national broadcasters will perform. Two semi-finals are held to whittle the number of songs down to 26.
    • The 10 entries with the most points from each semi advance to the Grand Final.
    • 5 entries (The Big Five) perform in the semis, but automatically advance to the Grand Final.
    • The performer from the previous year's winning nation automatically advances to the Grand Final.
  • The Big Five are France, Germany, Italy, Spain, and the United Kingdom. General wisdom says they get a free pass to the grand final as they foot the lion's share of the funding for the entire show.
  • There is (currently) no "official" language to songs in Eurovision
    • Previous years did have language restrictions
    • Many songs feature multiple languages
    • Some songs have been sung in fictional languages
    • Yes, English is a popular choice in Eurovision
