What's this all about?

The Eurovision Song Contest (ESC or just Eurovision) is a yearly, international song competition between members of the European Broadcasting Union. Each broadcaster internally chooses both a song and a performer to send to Eurovision, typically (and certainly not always) by a professional jury or a televised public vote (or both). More from eurovision.tv

Since voting in the Contest is only available to viewers in participating member countries, I've built this little application to allow fans in non-EBU locations to be able to hold their own, unofficial vote.

Go here to download the official Eurovision app

Its all for fun, folk! Numbers in this app are in no way connected to, nor will they reflect officially cast votes.

More about Eurovision

Here are the answers to some of the biggest FAQs I get. For more information, visit the official Eurovision website.

  • The first Eurovision Song Contest was held in 1956 between 7 nations.
  • The Contest is (typically) hosted/broadcast/produced by the broadcaster from previous year's winner's country. The 2019 contest is in Tel Aviv and produced by KAN as Israel won in 2018.
  • In 2019, participants from 41 national broadcasters will perform. Two semi-finals are held to whittle the number of songs down to 26.
    • The 10 entries with the most points from each semi advance to the Grand Final.
    • 5 entries (The Big Five) perform in the semis, but automatically advance to the Grand Final.
    • The country whose entry won the previous year automatically advances to the Grand Final
    • 10 + 10 + 5 + 1 = 26
  • The Big Five are France, Germany, Italy, Spain, and the United Kingdom. Basically, they get a free pass to the grand final as they foot the lion's share of the funding for the production of the show. (That's all I have to say about that)
  • There is (currently) no "official" language to songs in Eurovision
    • Previous years did have language restrictions
    • Many songs feature multiple languages
    • Some songs have been sung in fictional languages
    • Yes, English is a popular choice in Eurovision.
